Methodology

Three dimensions, one number

01

A long, healthy life

Measured by life expectancy at birth, normalised against a 20–85 year range set by the lowest and highest life expectancies observed globally.

02

Knowledge

The average of mean years of schooling actually completed by adults, and the years of schooling a child entering school today can expect to receive.

03

A decent standard of living

Gross National Income per capita, adjusted for purchasing power, and log-transformed so that income gains matter less once basic needs are already met.

HDI = 3√( Health × Education × Income ) the geometric mean penalises a nation whenever any single dimension lags behind

Under the hood

A model trained on the mechanics of the index itself

Rather than memorising a fixed lookup table, a classifier was trained on thousands of simulated national profiles spanning the full range of the four indicators, learning the same non-linear, compensating relationships that the official HDI formula encodes.
